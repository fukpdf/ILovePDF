How to Redact a PDF Online Free — Black Out Sensitive Info

Sharing a contract publicly but need to hide the dollar figures? Sending a court document where personal details must be obscured? Highlighting in your PDF reader doesn't cut it — anyone can remove a highlight. Redaction permanently covers content with an opaque rectangle that can't be unmasked.

The Redact PDF tool stamps black rectangles over any region you specify. Here's how.

Step-by-step: how to use Redact PDF

  1. Open the tool. Visit ilovepdf.cyou/redact-pdf.
  2. Upload the PDF. Drop the file. The tool reads dimensions automatically.
  3. Set redaction area. Enter X/Y position and width/height as percentages, e.g. X=10, Y=40, W=30, H=10.
  4. Choose pages. Type a page number (e.g. 1) or all to redact every page.
  5. Process and download. The redacted PDF saves as ILovePDF-redact.pdf.

Pro tips for the best results

1. Test on a copy

Always redact on a duplicate file — never on the only copy of the original.

2. Use precise percentages

Measure the target area in your viewer and translate to percentage of page width/height.

3. Combine with OCR removal

For text-searchable PDFs, also rasterise via PDF to JPG then back to PDF to remove residual text.

4. Verify before sharing

Always open the redacted PDF in a fresh viewer and try to copy the redacted area to confirm nothing remains.

Frequently asked questions

Can the redaction be reversed?

No. The black rectangles are rendered into the page — they can't be peeled away.

Does it remove the underlying text from the PDF stream?

The rectangle covers the visible content. For maximum security against text-extraction tools, also rasterise the PDF after redacting.

Can I use a colour other than black?

Currently black only. Colour customisation is planned.

Can I redact multiple areas at once?

One area per run. Process the file multiple times for multiple redactions.
