Frequently asked questions

What can I encode in a QR code?

URLs, plain text, email addresses, phone numbers, SMS messages, and Wi-Fi credentials (SSID + password). All common QR content formats are supported.

What error correction level should I use?

Level M (15% recovery) works for most use cases. Use Level H (30%) if the code may be partially covered, placed on a curved surface, or used in dirty/industrial environments. Level L produces the smallest code but is fragile.

What is the difference between PNG and SVG output?

PNG is a pixel-based format — perfect for digital sharing, web use, and most print jobs where you know the final size. SVG is vector-based — it scales to any size without pixelation, making it ideal for large-format printing, laser cutting, or embroidery.

Can I use a custom color?

Yes. Use the foreground and background color pickers to match any brand palette. Ensure the foreground is significantly darker than the background for reliable scanning. Avoid low-contrast combinations like light gray on white.

Is the QR code generator free?

Completely free. It runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent to any server, no account is required, and there are no usage limits.
