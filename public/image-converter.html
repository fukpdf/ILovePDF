Which format should I use?

JPEG (.jpg)

Best for photographs and realistic images. Lossy compression gives very small files. Does not support transparency. Universally compatible.

PNG (.png)

Best for graphics, screenshots, and images requiring transparency. Lossless — no quality degradation but larger file sizes than JPEG.

WebP (.webp)

Google's modern format. 25–35% smaller than JPEG at equivalent quality, supports transparency like PNG. Best for web use. Widely supported in modern browsers.

Frequently asked questions

Can I convert multiple images at once?

Yes — add as many images as you like. They are all converted and downloaded individually when you click Convert.

Will converting PNG to JPEG lose transparency?

Yes — JPEG does not support transparency. Transparent areas are filled with white when converting to JPEG. Convert to WebP instead if you need to keep transparency.

What does quality do for PNG?

PNG is lossless — quality has no effect on the visual output. All pixel information is preserved regardless of the quality setting. To reduce PNG file size, convert to WebP or JPEG instead.

Are my images uploaded anywhere?

No — Image Converter uses the HTML5 Canvas API to re-encode images directly in your browser. Nothing leaves your device.

Is it free?
