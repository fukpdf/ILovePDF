Frequently asked questions

Which formats can I compress?

JPEG, PNG, and WebP. JPEG and WebP are lossy formats — a quality setting of 75–85 gives excellent results with 30–70% size reduction. PNG uses lossless compression internally so it can't be reduced as aggressively with a quality slider, but converting to WebP or JPEG will significantly shrink PNG files.

What quality level should I use?

Quality 80 is the industry default for web photos — imperceptible quality loss with significant size reduction. Use 90+ for photography work or print. Use 60–70 when bandwidth is critical and subtle artefacts are acceptable (thumbnails, previews).

Are my images uploaded anywhere?

No. Image Compressor uses the browser's built-in Canvas API to re-encode your image locally. Your files never leave your device.

Can I convert format while compressing?

Yes — use the Output Format dropdown. Converting a PNG to JPEG or WebP often produces the largest size reduction since PNG is lossless.
