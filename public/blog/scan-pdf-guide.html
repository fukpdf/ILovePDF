How to Scan to PDF Online — Convert Image Scans to PDF

Your phone camera is a perfectly good scanner — for receipts, contracts, sticky notes, whiteboards, anything. But sharing 12 individual JPG photos is annoying. Combining them into a single, properly-oriented PDF is the right format for archiving and sharing.

The Scan to PDF tool converts a batch of scanned images (.jpg, .jpeg, .png) into a single PDF. Here's how.

Step-by-step: how to use Scan to PDF

  1. Open the tool. Visit ilovepdf.cyou/scan-pdf.
  2. Upload your scan images. Drag and drop multiple .jpg/.png files. They become PDF pages in upload order.
  3. Reorder pages. Drag the thumbnails into the right sequence before processing.
  4. Process. Each image becomes a single PDF page at original resolution.
  5. Download. Save the result as ILovePDF-scan-to-pdf.pdf.

Why use Scan to PDF?

  • Phone-friendly. Take photos with your phone, upload them in the browser, get a tidy PDF.
  • Single shareable file. Email or upload one PDF instead of attaching 10 separate images.

Pro tips for the best results

1. Use natural light

Phone "scans" turn out best in even, bright lighting. Avoid harsh shadows.

2. Crop and rotate first

Use Crop Image and Rotate PDF as needed for clean output.

3. OCR for searchable text

Run the result through OCR PDF to add a searchable text layer.

4. Compress before emailing

High-res phone photos make heavy PDFs. Run through Compress PDF.

Frequently asked questions

How many images can I scan?

No hard cap on count. Each image just needs to be under 100 MB.

Can I make the PDF text-searchable?

Yes — run the resulting PDF through OCR PDF to extract text.

What image formats are supported?

JPG, JPEG and PNG. HEIC photos should be converted to JPG first.

Will quality be lost?

No. Images are embedded at their original resolution into the PDF.
