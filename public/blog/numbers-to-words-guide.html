Numbers to Words Converter — Free Online Number Spelling

Writing a cheque, drafting a contract, or filling out a legal form often requires numbers spelled out as words — "One Thousand Two Hundred Fifty Dollars and 00/100" instead of "$1,250.00". Doing this by hand is slow and error-prone, especially for large amounts.

The Numbers to Words tool converts any number into words instantly, with options for case style and currency formatting.

Step-by-step: how to use Numbers to Words

  1. Open the tool. Visit ilovepdf.cyou/numbers-to-words.
  2. Enter your number. Type the number — integer or decimal, e.g. 12345.67.
  3. Choose Words or Currency. Words gives you "twelve thousand three hundred forty-five point six seven". Currency gives you "Twelve Thousand Three Hundred Forty-Five Dollars and 67/100".
  4. Pick a case. Lowercase, uppercase, title case or sentence case — to match the formatting of your document.
  5. Copy and paste. Hit Convert, then copy the result straight into your cheque, contract or invoice.

Pro tips for the best results

1. Use Currency mode for cheques

Currency mode formats the result as "Amount Dollars and XX/100" — exactly what banks expect.

2. Title Case for formal docs

Title case looks most professional in formal contract language.

3. Verify large amounts twice

For very large numbers, sanity-check the digit count before relying on the words form.

4. Combine with Excel to PDF

For batch invoice generation, build numbers in Excel then export with Excel to PDF.

Frequently asked questions

What's the largest number supported?

Numbers up to trillions are supported. Decimals work too.

Can I use other currencies?

The default is Dollars. Generic Words mode works for any currency context.

Does it work for negative numbers?

Yes — negatives are prefixed with "negative" or "minus" as appropriate.

Is there an API?

Currently a UI tool only. API access is on the roadmap for businesses needing batch conversions.
