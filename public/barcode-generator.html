Barcode formats explained

Code 128

Encodes all 128 ASCII characters. Ideal for general inventory, shipping, and logistics. No length restriction.

EAN-13

International retail barcode found on consumer products worldwide. Requires a 12- or 13-digit number.

EAN-8

Compact retail barcode for small products where EAN-13 is too large. Encodes 7–8 digits.

UPC-A

North American retail standard. Encodes 11 digits + check digit. Required for US grocery and retail.

ITF-14

Shipping container standard. Encodes 14 digits. Common on outer cartons and wholesale packaging.

Code 39

Alphanumeric barcode widely used in industrial, healthcare, and government sectors. Self-checking.

Frequently asked questions

Which format should I use for retail products?

Use EAN-13 for international retail (most of the world) or UPC-A for North America. Both require a valid GTIN from a registered GS1 company prefix to sell in stores.

Can I encode letters in a barcode?

Yes — Code128 and Code39 both support alphanumeric data. Code128 handles the full ASCII character set while Code39 is limited to uppercase letters, digits, and a few symbols.

Why is my EAN-13 showing an error?

EAN-13 requires exactly 12 or 13 digits. The 13th digit is the check digit — if you supply 12 digits, it is calculated automatically. Spaces, letters, and dashes are not allowed.
