Why use Excel to PDF?

  • Locked output. Numbers can't be accidentally changed once locked into PDF.
  • Print-ready. Predictable page breaks — no more mystery row chops on the recipient's printer.
  • Smaller files. Workbooks with formulas often shrink dramatically when only the rendered values are stored.
  • Universal viewing. PDF opens everywhere — Excel doesn't.

Frequently asked questions

Are formulas preserved?

No — PDFs store the computed values, not the underlying formulas. Keep your .xlsx as the master copy.

Will every sheet be exported?

Yes — each visible sheet becomes one or more PDF pages.

Can I export hidden sheets?

Hidden sheets are skipped by default. Unhide them in Excel before converting if needed.

How are charts handled?

Charts are rasterised into the PDF and look identical to the Excel preview.
