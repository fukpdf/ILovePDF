How to Sign a PDF Online for Free — Digital Signature Guide

Printing a contract just to sign it, scanning the signed page, then emailing the resulting PDF — it's 2025, and it's ridiculous. A digital signature can be added in your browser in under a minute, and it's legally binding in most jurisdictions for everyday business agreements.

The Sign PDF tool stamps a clean text-based signature onto any page. Here's how.

Step-by-step: how to use Sign PDF

  1. Open the tool. Visit ilovepdf.cyou/sign-pdf.
  2. Upload your PDF. Drag and drop the document you need to sign.
  3. Type your signature. Enter your full name (or signature wording) in the Signature field.
  4. Pick the page. Specify which page to sign. Leave blank to sign the last page (the most common spot).
  5. Process and download. The signed PDF saves as ILovePDF-sign.pdf.

Frequently asked questions

Is a typed signature legally valid?

For most everyday agreements (employment, freelance, NDAs), typed signatures are accepted under ESIGN and eIDAS laws. High-stakes contracts may require certified e-signatures.

Can I draw my signature?

Currently text-based only. Drawn signature support is on the roadmap.

Where on the page is the signature placed?

It's anchored near the bottom of the chosen page by default — the typical signature line location.

Can multiple people sign?

Run the tool again on the signed file to add a second signature on a different page.
