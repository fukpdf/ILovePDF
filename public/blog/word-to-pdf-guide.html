Why use Word to PDF?

  • Universal format. PDF renders identically on every device — no font surprises, no layout shifts.

Pro tips for the best results

1. Embed your fonts in Word first

Use Word's "Embed fonts in the file" option before exporting if you used a custom font.

2. Compress after converting

Use Compress PDF if your Word doc has large embedded images.

3. Add watermark or page numbers

Use Watermark PDF or Add Page Numbers for the final touch.

4. Protect with a password

Lock sensitive PDFs with Protect PDF before emailing.

Frequently asked questions

Will my fonts look the same?

Standard fonts (Calibri, Times, Arial) are preserved. Custom fonts must be embedded in the source Word file.

Are images preserved?

Yes — embedded images render at full quality in the resulting PDF.

Can I convert .doc files (older format)?

Yes, both .doc and .docx are supported.

Does it preserve hyperlinks?

Yes — clickable hyperlinks survive the conversion.
